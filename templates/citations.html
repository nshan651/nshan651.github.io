Citations Page

Hemingway, Ernest (1899-1961)

Cooke, Douglas. "Hemingway, Ernest (1899–1961)." The St. James Encyclopedia of Popular Culture Vol. 2 2nd ed. Pages 642-646. 2013; Accessed October 24, 2020. From Gale Ebooks; Accessed through luc.edu

This article from the Encyclopedia of Popular Culture focuses on Ernest Hemingway’s writing style and literary contributions. The article talks about each of Hemingway’s written works in chronological order while also providing background context for each, such as public reception. This article appears to be free of any apparent biases, the author seems to do a good job of detailing Hemingway’s publishing history with an objective perspective in mind

The Last Days of Ernest Hemingway

Hotchner, A.E. “The Last Days of Ernest of Ernest Hemingway.” ProQuest Historical Newspapers: The Chicago Tribune. Page J31. October 23, 1966; Accessed October 25, 2020. From ProQuest Historical Newspapers

This article is a part of the ProQuest Historical Newspaper collection. It is an article from the Chicago Tribune published on October 23, 1966 – five years after Hemingway’s death. It is published by A.E. Hotchner, an associate of Hemingway’s, and the eventual author of his biography. The article recounts Hotchner’s perspective on Hemingway’s final moments.This will be an excellent resource in the section of my website where I plan to talk about Hemingway’s final moments and summarize his career in retrospect. It is a credible, first-hand source that will be interesting to include.

Hemingway on War and Its Aftermath

Putnam, Thomas. “Hemingway on War and Its Aftermath.” Prologue Magazine – U.S. National Archives and Records Administration, Vol. 38, #1. Spring 2006; Accessed October 25, 2020. From the National Archives

This article from Prologue Magazine details Hemingway’s experience in the First World War, and how it influenced his future literary works. This article seems to be reliable; it is from a journal that is part of the US National Archives and Records Administration, and it includes citations and reviews.

Writer, Novelist, Nobel Prize Recipient

Reynolds, Michael. “Hemingway, Ernest (1899-1961), writer, novelist, Nobel Prize recipient.” The Oxford Encyclopedia of American Cultural and Intellectual History. 2013; Accessed October 24, 2020. From the Oxford Encyclopedia

This article, taken from the Oxford Encyclopedia of American Culture, gives a very brief overview of Hemingway’s life and writing career. It begins with his early journalism stint, his war experience, and his early association with Modernist writers. It talks briefly about his short stories, then his novels, and finally his posthumously published works. This article appears to be a veritable, trustworthy source that has been edited.

"Modernism"

Singal, Daniel. “Modernism”. The Oxford Encyclopedia of American Cultural and Intellectual History. 2013; Accessed October 24, 2020. From the Oxford Encyclopedia

This article, also taken from the Oxford Encyclopedia, lays out the history and ideas behind the Modernist movement. It talks about the distinction between “Modernism” and “Modernization”, some of the notable Modernist writers, and Modernist beliefs and culture. The source seems to be a credible, edited source.

On the Influence of Naturalism on American Literature

Zhang, Xiaofen. “On the Influence of Naturalism on American Literature”. Education Resources Information Center, Vol. 3, #2. June 2010; Accessed October 24, 2020. From the Education Resources Information Center

This article, taken from the ERIC Institute of Education Sciences collection, talks about the rise of the Naturalist movement, and specifically how it influenced American Literature in the late 19th and early-to-mid 20th century. It includes a substantial section on Hemingway. This article seems to be trustworthy; it is a peer reviewed article published on the Education Resources Information Center (ERIC), which is an online digital library sponsored by the United States Department of Education.
